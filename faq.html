FAQ

Q1. What is a Menstrual Cup?
A menstrual cup is a small, bell-shaped cup that is required to be inserted into the vagina to collect menstrual blood during a period. It’s a feminine hygiene device and is a great alternative to pads and tampons as it effectively collects the menstrual fluid and prevents any leakage of it onto the clothes.
Q2. What is a Menstrual Cup made of?
A menstrual cup is made up of 100% medical-grade silicone, latex rubber or plastic and has a shape like that of a bell with a stem.
Q3. Are Menstrual Cups safe to use?
Since they’re not prone to dampness, outbreaks of vaginal candidiasis, cystitis or toxic shock syndrome they’re considered to be much safer than tampons.
Q4. How does a Menstrual Cup work?
A Menstrual cup works on the principle of collecting the menstruating fluid instead of soaking it and therefore, requires emptying after every few hours to reuse them.
Q5. How to use/insert a menstrual cup?
Start with your cup open. Squeeze the cup between your fingers and thumbs so that the lips touch together. Now fold the cup from inside making a C/U shape depending on how you are holding it. Hold it firmly in the same position and insert.

You are required to be patient as it will take time and a few attempts to find out that one right fold the one you are most comfortable with. Check our guide to finding your best fold/method to insert.
Q6. How will I know if the cup is inserted properly?
When inserted properly, you should be able to feel the cup around your vaginal walls and notice no leakage. The cup should not be protruding out from your vagina. If the stem protrudes, it can be trimmed. If the bottom of the cup protrudes, the cup needs to be placed higher or is too long for you.
Q7. How will I if the cup is full?
You can wear it without any fear of leakage for up to 12 hours (6-8 hours in case of heavy flow) but we recommend checking it at least every 4 hours.
Q8. Why should I clean my Menstrual Cup?
Cup left unclean becomes susceptible to bacteria growth which can lead to different vaginal infections such as Toxic Shock Syndrome (TSS), so don’t put you to serious health risks
Q9. How to wash my Menstrual Cup for reuse?
You can simply empty the cup, wash it with a disinfectant or a normal soap and water and insert it again to reuse.

If possible, use a mild liquid soap which is unscented, oil-free and non-antibacterial in nature. Harsh chemicals can potentially change the PH level of your cup and cause irritation when reinserted.

Soaps that contain some natural oils can de-nature the medical silicone and soften it slightly.
Q10. How to store my Menstrual Cup at the end of the menstrual cycle?
Properly sterilize your menstrual cup in boiling water before and after each menstrual cycle. To sterilize, dip it in hot water for 5-10 minutes, take it out and allow it to get air dry. You can also keep in the sun to dry.

To store your cup to reuse, keep it in a breathable pouch or container. Do not keep your cup in an airtight container or a plastic bag as the air should flow around the cup allowing the cup to dry properly.
Q11. How can I clean my Menstrual Cup in public restrooms or while travelling?
Simply empty the contents into the toilet seat, rinse the cup over the toilet with water and wipe clean with some toilet paper or a wet towel or with a menstrual cup wipe (if available).
Q12. Can I use a menstrual cup if I experience heavy flow during periods?
Yes, a menstrual cup holds more fluid than even a tampon can absorb. It helps you to store fluid for at least 6-8 hours even if you’re having a heavy flow. You may have to empty it every 3-4 hours in case you experience an exceptionally heavy flow.
Q13. Can I use a menstrual cup if I’m a virgin?
Yes, many girls who are first starting to menstruate prefer menstrual cups over sanitary pads or tampons. If you’re a teenager who never had sexual intercourse then size ‘small’ will be the best option for you. A teenager can start using the ‘small’ size cup as soon as she gets her period for the first time.
Q14. Can I use menstrual cup during any sport or work out activity?
Yes, absolutely. Whether you’re hitting the gym or going for your dance classes, menstrual cups are much more comfortable and convenient to use. You can use them during your day-to-day physical activities, work out, running, dancing, travelling without any worries of leakage.
Q15. Will my menstrual cup leak?
Your menstrual cup can leak due to the following reasons:

Incorrect Insertion: Take it out and try reinserting it and ensure the cup opens completely within.

Wrong size: If you’re using a small size cup, it will not seal correctly around the vaginal walls.
Q16. What is the life of a menstrual cup?
If cleaned and stored properly after every menstrual cycle, an iShield cup can last up to 8 years. However, if you see any sign of deterioration, it’s time to replace your cup.
Q17. Can I use a menstrual cup overnight?
Yes. Menstrual cups can be worn up to 12 hours depending on the amount of flow and are fit for overnight use.
Q18. Can I use a menstrual cup during intercourse?
No, menstrual cups are not designed to be used while having sexual intercourse.
Q19. Can I pee with the cup in?
Cups do not hinder your normal functions – peeing or pooping with exception to sex. If you feel that cup is coming out while peeing/pooping, don’t worry. You can just nudge it back to its position.
